Dear Martin

A learning page about the novel by Nic Stone

About the Author

Nic Stone is an American writer. She writes books for young people. Her stories often talk about racism, justice, and growing up.

Dear Martin was published in 2018.

Summary

The book is about a Black teenager named Justyce McAllister. He is a good student and wants to become a lawyer.

One day, Justyce has a bad experience with the police. After that, he starts writing letters to Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. In these letters, he talks about racism, fear, and unfair treatment.

The story shows how hard life can be for Black teenagers in America.

Main Themes

  • Racism
  • Justice and injustice
  • Police violence
  • Identity
  • Friendship

Main Characters

  • Justyce McAllister: The main character. Smart and thoughtful.
  • Quan Banks: Justyce’s childhood friend.
  • Manny Rivers: A classmate who causes problems.
  • Jared: Justyce’s best friend from school.

Important Quote

“I can’t change where I come from, but I can change where I’m going.”

This quote shows that Justyce wants a better future, even if his past was difficult.

Why Is This Book Important?

Dear Martin helps readers understand racism and unfair treatment. It makes people think about society and their own actions.

The book is still important today.
